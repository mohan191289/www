Roles and Responsibilities

Volunteers acts as administrative staff and program support. They help create, grow and propel relationships with mentors by taking responsibility for different roles.

  • Collect Mentee and Mentor data
  • Make the program accessible to both Mentors and Mentee ensuring its adaptability to their specific dynamic
  • Conduct Meetings
  • Take minutes at meetings
  • Review the agenda and accompanying children to meeting
  • Stick to the agenda during meetings
  • Maintaining records and Information
  • Document all interaction between a Mentor and Mentee
  • Send the reports of every interaction through tracker
  • Update your tracker within three hours from the commencement of meet
  • Intimate the coordinators and the centre heads before conducting every meet
  • Try to build a good rapport with mentor and mentee
  • Request for approval of a new policy / change in existing policy

Roles and Responsibilities

Mentor must be willing to accept the commitment of time and energy to provide support for a new mentee. A clear understanding of the mentoring process will go a long way towards assuring success. You will need to maintain support and open communication. You will also:

  • Intimate contact with the mentee
  • Meet mentee at least once in a month
  • Be a good listener
  • Meet at scheduled times
  • Maintain confidentiality
  • Give constructive feedback
  • Promote mentees skill and creativity
  • Share experience based cultural values
  • Help the mentee to move towards goal
  • Build a professional network for mentee
  • Maintain a positive attitude
  • Encourage independence
  • Create access to resources (Financial & Non - Financial)
  • Remember there is most often more than one way to reach a goal. Support the mentee to the best of your ability, but remember the project success or failure of the mentee is in the hands of the mentor.
